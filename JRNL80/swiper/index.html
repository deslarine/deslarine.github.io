Artificial Reefs Being Constructed in Long Island

By Ethan Marshall and Desiree Jaime

We’re giving an old bridge a second life as a home for diverse marine life. It’s our latest effort to protect and foster the natural environment around Long Island. https://t.co/wIZP7BEZPC

— Andrew Cuomo (@NYGovCuomo) April 30, 2018

Artificial reefs are underwater structures that were initially man-made, like, for example, a sunken ship.

According to Joey Rocco, 57, a diver certified by the Professional Association of Diving Instructors (PADI), the ecosystem may benefit the most from the artificial reefs.

"The artificial reefs would help provide more oxygen and clean up the water that's already there," Rocco said.

The reefs would help in attracting more marine life.

In the end, we should have a cleaner ocean and healthier ocean," Rocco said.

According to New York State Governor Andrew Cuomo, parts of the old Tappan Zee Bridge will be used in the reef's construction.
